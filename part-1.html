Part I: Human, Organisational & Regulatory Aspects

Understanding governance, regulations, human factors, and privacy in cybersecurity

1Risk Management & Governance

Overview

Risk management and governance address how organizations systematically identify, measure, and manage cybersecurity risks. This knowledge area emphasizes organizational approaches to understanding and mitigating security threats.

Key Topics
  • Risk Assessment Methods: Quantitative and qualitative approaches to evaluating security risks
  • Governance Frameworks: ISO 27005, NIST, and organizational risk management practices
  • Asset Management: Identifying and valuing critical assets
  • Threat & Vulnerability Analysis: Understanding attack vectors and system weaknesses
  • Risk Metrics & KPIs: Measuring and monitoring security performance
  • Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery: Planning for security incidents
  • Incident Response Planning: Procedures for detecting and responding to breaches
Source Citation: CyBOK v1.1.0 - Risk Management & Governance section (relevant content spans multiple chapters in the PDF). Crown Copyright 2021.

2Law & Regulation

Overview

This knowledge area covers the legal and regulatory frameworks that govern cybersecurity, including laws related to cyber crimes, data protection, international agreements, and export controls on cryptographic technologies.

Key Topics
  • Cybercrime Laws: Computer Misuse Acts, unauthorized access provisions, DDoS legislation
  • Data Protection Regulations: GDPR, privacy laws, data breach notification requirements
  • International Frameworks: Budapest Convention on Cybercrime, mutual legal assistance treaties
  • Intellectual Property: Copyright, patents, trade secrets in digital context
  • Cryptography Export Controls: Restrictions on exporting encryption technologies
  • Digital Contracts & Signatures: Electronic evidence and digital signatures in law
  • Liability & Compliance: Security audit obligations, reporting requirements
Source Citation: CyBOK v1.1.0 - Law & Regulation section. Crown Copyright 2021.

3Human Factors

Overview

Human factors recognizes that people are integral to cybersecurity - not merely a "weak link." This area examines how psychology, usability, and organizational behavior affect security effectiveness.

Key Topics
  • Security Psychology: Understanding user behavior, motivation, and compliance
  • Usability & UX Security: Designing security that users can and will use correctly
  • Password Policies: Balancing security requirements with human capability
  • Security Awareness Training: Effective education and behavior change programs
  • Social Engineering: Manipulation techniques targeting human trust
  • Insider Threats: Understanding, managing, and detecting malicious insiders
  • Secure Culture: Building organizational security mindsets and practices
Source Citation: CyBOK v1.1.0 - Human Factors section. Crown Copyright 2021.

4Privacy & Online Rights

Overview

Privacy is a fundamental human right. This knowledge area examines both technical and policy approaches to protecting individual privacy in digital systems, including Privacy by Design principles.

Key Topics
  • Privacy Threats: Data collection, tracking, surveillance, profiling
  • Privacy by Design: Building privacy into system architecture from the start
  • Data Anonymization & Pseudonymization: Techniques to de-identify personal information
  • Privacy-Enhancing Technologies (PETs): Differential privacy, homomorphic encryption, federated learning
  • Regulatory Compliance: GDPR, privacy laws, consent requirements
  • Online Rights & Digital Freedoms: Freedom of expression, access rights
  • Data Breach Response: Notification, remediation, individual rights
Source Citation: CyBOK v1.1.0 - Privacy & Online Rights section. Crown Copyright 2021.
